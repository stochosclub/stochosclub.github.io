What does 'Employer of Record' mean?

Rivermate acts as an Employer of Record for your employees. This means that Rivermate will be the legal employer as far as the government, tax and employment authorities are concerned.

As an Employer of Record, we are responsible for:

  • Ensuring the employment of your employees complies with local labor laws
  • Processing local payroll
  • Tax filings connected to employment
  • Issuing payslips to employees
  • Distributing salary payments
  • Payments to the local tax authorities

You are responsible for:

  • Recruitment of your own workers
  • Day-to-day management of the employees
  • Following any guidance on employment, legal and HR obligations in the employee's country
  • Ensuring the payments for payroll of your team is paid to Rivermate on time
  • Distributing salary payments
  • Payments to the local tax authorities
Can I cancel anytime?
Our pricing plan is a monthly plan, which means you can cancel any time. The payments will stop as soon as you offboard a contractor or an employee.

No minimum costs, no contracts, no hidden costs. Just simple, transparent pricing.
How does invoicing and billing work?
On the 10th of every month, we issue you with an invoice bill. This includes payroll, taxes, benefits, expenses, and any bonuses or commissions. Within this invoice, you will also find our management fee which is how much we charge for a contractor or employee. The invoice will be in your home currency.

You pay Rivermate, and we will distribute the payments to your team in their currency. We also file and pay their taxes compliantly in their home country.
What is the difference between a contractor and an employee
An independent contractor is a worker outside of the business. They are “independent” which means that they could run their own businesses, or do work outside your company. A contractor is hired by a company to perform tasks for the business. Typically, a contractor is given a discrete project or a set of tasks to complete with minimal direct supervision.

An employee is employed to do any work-for-hire or reward under an employment agreement. The hire or reward is almost always a wage or salary. Employees must get:
  • At least the minimum wage
  • Holiday and leave entitlements
  • An employment agreement
